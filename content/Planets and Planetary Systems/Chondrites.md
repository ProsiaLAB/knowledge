Chondrites are a predominately non-metallic class of meteorites with numerous embedded round grain-like objects (*chondrules*), which have not undergone appreciable melting or differentiation.

Exposure to intense heat melts the embedded chondrules, implying that chondritic meteorites were immediately subjected cooling after their formation, preserving the chondrules and thus, they represent the most primitive of all [[Meteorites|meteorites]].
